What I do

Scientific Research

Over three years of experience in scientific research in both my undergraduate and graduate degrees which culminated in two first author publications (under review). My undergraduate research work was in experimental plasma physics, studying extreme state physics at XSPL. My graduate thesis work at the Labbe Lab focused on biofuels, specifically studying the combustion behavior of soot-reducing diesel additives with computational chemistry methods.

Data Science

Industry and academic experience with relational databases, namely with Microsoft SQL Server. My previous role as a Data Scientist involved querying, importing, analyzing, and extracting insights on sales data using Python. The data was made accessible by deploying a business intelligence app that showcased our products compared to those of competitros using web scraping tools like Beautiful Soup and Selenium.

Machine Learning in Python

I've leveraged Python libraries (numpy, pandas, sklearn) in my research work to build classical machine learning models (i.e. KNN, ensemble methods, regressors) to extract knowledge from experiments. In personal projects, I've used basic NLP libraries (Vader, TextBlob) to perform sentiment analysis on online posts, and have build extensions to open-spurce neural networks codes to enable Chess engines to play the Millenium 3D Chess variation.

Who I am

Hi, I'm Andres Felipe Cano Botero, I was born in Colombia and raised in Panama. I'm a highly curious individual who loves science and is constantly in the quest of learning new skills, this motivated me to get a Bachelor's in Science in Astrophysics from the University of Rochester. During my degree I learned the key problem-solving and computational skills that I would later use as a Data Scientist for Opera Inversiones, a real estate and food market investment firm. During my time at Opera, I learned about relational databases, data science with Python, and machine learning algorithms. In August of 2020, I decided to further develop my computational and scientific background by pursuing a Master's in Science in Mechanical Engineering at CU Boulder

Aside from taking interesting coursework such as High Performance Computing, Machine Learning Algorithms, and Data Mining, my masters was shaped by my research work at the Labbe Lab, which culminated with the writing of my thesis: Alkyl end group effects on the thermal decomposition of oxymethylene ether fuel additives and two first-author publications (under review). I recently graduated and I'm looking for full-time opportunities where I can apply, and further develop, my computational skills to gather meaningful insight from data.
